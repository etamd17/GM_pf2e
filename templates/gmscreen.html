Common Conditions

Off-Guard (Flat-Footed) ▼
Takes a –2 circumstance penalty to AC.
Frightened [X] ▼
Takes a status penalty equal to X to all checks and DCs. Decreases by 1 at the end of your turn.
Sickened [X] ▼
Takes a status penalty equal to X to all checks and DCs. Cannot willingly ingest anything. Spend an action retching (Fort save vs effect DC) to reduce.
Prone ▼
Off-Guard. Take a –2 circumstance penalty to attack rolls. Only move action allowed is Crawl. Standing up costs 1 action.
Concealed / Hidden ▼
Concealed: DC 5 flat check to target.

Hidden: Off-Guard to you. DC 11 flat check to target. You know their space.

Undetected: Off-Guard to you. You do not know their space. Must guess square to attack (DC 11 flat check).

Dying & Recovery

When reduced to 0 HP, move your initiative to directly before the creature that downed you, and gain Dying 1 (or Dying 2 if critical hit/failure).

  • Recovery Check: Flat check DC 10 + current Dying value.
    • Crit Success: Dying decreases by 2.
    • Success: Dying decreases by 1.
    • Failure: Dying increases by 1.
    • Crit Failure: Dying increases by 2.

When Dying reaches 0, you lose Dying and gain Wounded 1 (or increase Wounded by 1). If you take damage while Dying, increase Dying by 1 (or 2 for a crit).

Dead at Dying 4.

Elite & Weak Adjustments

Elite Adjustment (+1 Level)
  • +2 to AC, Saves, Attacks, DCs, Perception, Skills
  • +2 to Damage rolls
  • +10 to 30 HP (based on level)
Weak Adjustment (-1 Level)
  • -2 to AC, Saves, Attacks, DCs, Perception, Skills
  • -2 to Damage rolls
  • -10 to 30 HP (based on level)

Undead & Void Healing

  • Immunities Death effects, disease, poison, unconscious.
  • Void Healing Healed by void (negative) energy. Damaged by vitality (positive) energy.
  • Destroyed Automatically destroyed at 0 HP. (Do not use Dying rules for basic undead).

Save Result Tiers ±10 rule

Crit Success (beat DC by 10+ or nat 20) — no effect, or effect becomes Success.
Success — usually half damage; rider effects reduced.
Failure — full damage; rider effects apply.
Crit Failure (miss by 10+ or nat 1) — double damage, or worse rider effect.
